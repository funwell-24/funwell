Welcome to Sustainable Gardening

Discover how to create an environmentally friendly garden that supports local ecosystems while reducing your environmental impact.

Sustainable gardening offers numerous benefits:

  • Reduces water consumption
  • Supports pollinators and wildlife
  • Decreases reliance on chemicals
  • Improves soil health naturally

Water Conservation Techniques

Smart Watering Practices

Reduce your garden's water footprint with these effective strategies:

Key Methods:

Drip Irrigation
Delivers water directly to plant roots with minimal evaporation
Rainwater Harvesting
Collect and store rainwater in barrels for garden use
Mulching
Organic mulch retains soil moisture and regulates temperature

Composting Basics

Turning Waste into Garden Gold

Composting reduces landfill waste while creating nutrient-rich soil amendments.

Composting Do's and Don'ts:

  • Do add: Vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, yard waste
  • Don't add: Meat, dairy, oily foods, pet waste
  • Maintain a 3:1 ratio of browns (carbon) to greens (nitrogen)
  • Turn your pile regularly to aerate

Benefits of Native Plants

Working With Nature

Native plants are adapted to local conditions and support regional ecosystems.

Advantages of Native Plants:

  1. Require less water once established
  2. Need fewer fertilizers and pesticides
  3. Provide habitat for local wildlife
  4. Promote biodiversity
  5. Are naturally resistant to local pests

Organic Pest Control

Natural Solutions for Common Problems

Manage garden pests without harmful chemicals using these methods:

Common Garden Pests and Organic Solutions
Pest Organic Control Method
Aphids Ladybugs, neem oil spray, strong water blast
Slugs Diatomaceous earth, copper tape, beer traps
Tomato Hornworm Hand picking, Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt)
